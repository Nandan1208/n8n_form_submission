JD Writing Instructions

Use these to reduce back-and-forth & get faster approvals.
  • Be outcome-focused: describe what success looks like in 90 days, not just tasks.
  • Use clear structure: Summary → Responsibilities → Must-have → Nice-to-have.
  • Keep it realistic: avoid “10 tools + 10 years” for junior/mid roles.
  • Write in action verbs: Build, Own, Design, Deliver, Collaborate, Improve.
  • Limit responsibilities: 6–10 crisp bullets. Each bullet = one idea.
  • Skills should be searchable: use industry terms candidates actually use.
  • Remove biased language: avoid age/gender-coded phrases; keep inclusive wording.
  • Include constraints: shift timings, travel needs, weekend support, on-call, etc.
  • Location clarity: specify Remote/Hybrid/On-site + city/region + time zone if needed.
  • File hygiene: no images/screenshots; use headings and bullets; keep it skimmable.

Recommended JD Template (Quick)

Role: Title, Level, Team • Location: Mode + City
Summary: 2–4 lines impact statement
Responsibilities: 6–10 bullets
Must-have: 5–8 bullets (true requirements)
Nice-to-have: 3–5 bullets
Interview signals: what you’ll assess (optional)

Common Issues That Delay Approval

Vague responsibilities (“handle tasks”), missing location/work mode, unrealistic must-haves, no reporting line, or role not aligned with level.

Compliance Reminder

Do not include age, gender preference, marital status, or any discriminatory criteria. Keep eligibility based only on role-relevant skills and responsibilities.
